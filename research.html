Adaptive clothing for children with developmental disabilities



This project developed adaptive upper-body clothing for children with developmental disabilities by improving garment structures and dressing methods. The design aims to make dressing easier, more comfortable, and more independent for both children and caregivers.

김성영, 송지연, 홍은솔 학생 연구, “발달 장애 아동을 위한 상의 개발”

Running jacket for upper limb amputees



This project developed a running jacket for individuals with upper-limb amputations using a user-centered design approach. The jacket incorporates an accessible side-opening structure to support easier dressing while maintaining functionality, comfort, and a modern athletic appearance.

유혜원, 박배정 학생 연구, “상지 절단 장애인을 위한 러닝 재킷 개발”

New glove pattern drafting method

This project developed a new ergonomic glove pattern drafting method based on hand skin-surface changes and joint range of motion. Three-dimensional hand data were flattened into two-dimensional patterns, and the resulting prototypes were evaluated for fit, mobility, and clothing pressure.

Lee, S. M., Kim, M., & Park, J. (2022, October). An ergonomic glove pattern drafting method for hand assistive devices. Oral presented at the Annual Conference of International Textile and Apparel Association, Colorado, USA.

Cooperation with Gwanak-gu Welfare Center for People with Disabilities.













Warp-knitted SMA rehabilitation glove

This project developed a rehabilitation glove using warp-knitted shape memory alloy actuators to support hand movement in individuals with hemiplegia. The glove was designed based on user needs and hand-shape data, and its performance was evaluated through joint range of motion, grip force, skin temperature, and wearability tests.

Lee, S. M., Shin, G., Im, H., Oh, M., Avalos, S., Yoon, M. J., Kim, J. S., Park, J., & Ahn, S. H. (2024). Feasibility of a Warp-Knitted Shape Memory Alloy Rehabilitation Glove. Journal of Industrial Textiles

Collaboration with IDIM at Seoul National Univeristy and Rehabilitation Medicine, St. Vincent's Hospital, College of Medicine, The Catholic University of Korea









Rehabilitation gloves based on SMA

This project developed a soft wearable exoglove using knitted shape memory alloy actuators to assist hand rehabilitation. The glove design was based on three-dimensional hand analysis and user measurements, and its actuation performance, functional assistance, and ease of donning and doffing were systematically evaluated.

Lee, S. M., & Park, J. (2024). A soft wearable exoglove for rehabilitation assistance: A novel application of knitted Shape-Memory Alloy as a flexible actuator. Fashion and Textiles.

Lee, S. M., Jung, W. K., & Park, J. (2023). Mapping actuating performance of knitted Shape Memory Alloys and proposing design guidelines for assistive wearable applications. Journal of Industrial Textiles.









Electronic wrist bracelet

This project evaluated the wearability of an electronic wrist bracelet used in the criminal justice system through a multidimensional approach. Wrist mobility, daily activity performance, clothing pressure, and the air gap between the bracelet and the wrist were assessed to identify factors affecting long-term comfort and usability.

Lee, S. M., Lee, S. H ., & Park, J. (2022). A multidimensional approach to wearability assessment of an electronic wrist bracelet for the criminal justice system. Fashion and Textiles

Cooperation with Life Science Technology.






Multifunctional elbow brace

This project developed a multifunctional elbow brace using knitted shape memory alloy modules that provide both bending and compression functions. The prototype was evaluated through actuator performance tests and wearability assessments using skin temperature and blood flow measurements.

Jung, W. K., Lee, S. M., Park, J., & Ahn, S. H. (2022). Development and assessment of a knitted shape memory alloy-based multifunctional elbow brace. Journal of Industrial Textiles.

Collaboration with IDIM at Seoul National Univeristy.








4D hand gripping aid




This project developed a 4D hand-gripping aid using knitted shape memory alloy modules to assist finger flexion in older adults. The device was designed to provide lightweight and flexible gripping support, and its actuation performance and finger-bending assistance were experimentally evaluated.

Lee, S. M., Jung, W. K., Ahn, S. H., & Park, J. (2022). Development of a 4D hand gripping aid using a knitted Shape Memory Alloy and evaluation of finger-bending angles in elderly women. Fashion and Textiles

Collaboration with IDIM at Seoul National University
