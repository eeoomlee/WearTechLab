About

Soo-Min Lee, Ph.D.

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Clothing and Textiles at Kyung Hee University, where I lead the Wearable Design & Tech Convergence Lab.

My research explores how textile structures, smart materials, and human-centered design methods can be integrated into wearable products that support movement, rehabilitation, safety, thermal comfort, and daily living. I am particularly interested in translating material-level performance into body-worn systems that are usable, comfortable, and meaningful for real users.

Research Areas

From smart materials to wearable experiences

01

Smart Textiles & Actuators

Development and evaluation of textile-integrated actuation systems, including shape memory alloy structures, textile-based shape morphing, and responsive knitted systems.

02

Soft Wearable Robotics

Design of assistive and rehabilitative wearable systems that support hand, upper-limb, foot, and lower-limb movement while maintaining softness and wearability.

03

Wearable Ergonomics

Analysis of comfort, fit, motion, pressure, usability, and acceptance in clothing and wearable technology through human-centered methods.

04

Protective & Safety Wearables

User-centered wearable products for older adults, disaster-vulnerable populations, fall prevention, thermal safety, and personal safety support.

Selected Projects

Current and recent research themes

2025–2026

Intelligent Soft Exoskeletal Socks and User Needs Analysis

User-centered research on wearable safety devices for older adults in disaster-prone regions, focusing on needs, acceptance, usability, and dissemination.
