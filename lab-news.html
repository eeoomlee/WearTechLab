AI Expo Tokyo



Dr. Soo-Min Lee visited AI Expo Tokyo and NexTech Week 2024 to explore emerging technologies in artificial intelligence, robotics, and human-centered systems. The visit also included observations of innovative fashion, textile, and retail environments in Tokyo, providing inspiration for future wearable technology research.

Research Fair Awards in JBNU



Students from our Lab participated in the 2024 JBNU Research Fair and presented projects on user-centered apparel design, adaptive clothing, and sustainable fashion. Several student projects received awards in recognition of their creativity, research quality, and practical contributions.

Presentation at the Korea Society of Clothing and Textiles



Dr. Soo-Min Lee and her research team participated in the 2024 Fall Conference of the Korean Society of Clothing and Textiles held at Korea University. The team presented research on user-centered apparel design, including adaptive clothing for individuals with disabilities and functional running jackets.

Kick-off Ceremony of Wearability Evaluation Team



The Wearability Evaluation Team held its kick-off ceremony at Jeonbuk National University on September 12, 2024. In collaboration with K2 Korea, the team launched a project to develop ergonomic design recommendations for outdoor apparel through systematic wearability evaluation.

Workshop at Seoul National University



Dr. Soo-Min Lee participated in the 2024 BK21 Workshop for graduate students in the Department of Textiles, Merchandising and Fashion Design at Seoul National University. The workshop provided opportunities for career exploration, academic exchange, and networking among graduate students and researchers.

Appointed as Assistant Professor at JBNU



Dr. Soo-Min Lee was appointed as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Clothing and Textiles at Jeonbuk National University on March 1, 2024. The appointment ceremony marked the beginning of her academic role in wearable ergonomics, soft wearable robotics, and user-centered product development.
